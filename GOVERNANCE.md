# Governance

Steer the Build uses a maintainer-led, evidence-oriented governance model.

## Decision authority

The lead maintainer owns project scope, releases, terminology, and final editorial decisions. Maintainers may delegate review areas such as translation, testing, security, mobile, ML, or observability.

## Decision principles

1. Preserve the central thesis: AI can execute broadly, but humans own intent, risk, evidence, and consequences.
2. Prefer tested experience and reproducible counterexamples over popularity or tool marketing.
3. Keep claims scoped. Personal observations must not be presented as universal facts.
4. Separate stable principles from tool-specific guidance that can age quickly.
5. Never trade privacy, customer confidentiality, or security for a more impressive case study.
6. Keep the Chinese and English core meaning aligned, while allowing natural language adaptation.

## Contribution path

- Discussions: open questions, experience sharing, method debates, and early proposals.
- Issues: bounded content gaps, errors, and accepted work items.
- Pull requests: reviewable changes with evidence and a clear scope.

## Conflicts

When maintainers disagree, the preferred sequence is: clarify the disputed claim, identify required evidence, test or research where possible, document uncertainty, and defer publication rather than force false consensus.

## Changes to governance

Material governance changes require a pull request, a public rationale, and approval from the lead maintainer.
